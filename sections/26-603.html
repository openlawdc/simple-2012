Law 13-263, the "Protections from Predatory Lending and Mortgage Foreclosure Improvements Act of 2000", was introduced in Council and assigned Bill No. 13- 800, which was referred to the Committee on Economic Development. The Bill was adopted on first and second readings on November 8, 2000, and December 5, 2000, respectively. Signed by the Mayor on December 21, 2000, it was assigned Act No. 13-552 and transmitted to both Houses of Congress for its review. D.C. Law 13-263 became effective on April 3, 2001. Law 14-86, the "Protections from Predatory Lending and Mortgage Foreclosure Improvements Temporary Amendment Act of 2001", was introduced in Council and assigned Bill No. 14-417, which was referred to the Committee on Public Works and the Environment. The Bill was adopted on first and second readings on November 6, 2001, and December 4, 2001, respectively. Approved without the signature of the Mayor on December 21, 2001, it was assigned Act No. 14-206 and transmitted to both Houses of Congress for its review. D.C. Law 14-86 became effective on March 19, 2002.
