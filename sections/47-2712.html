REFUNDS

A refund of permit fees shall be made as follows:

the fee in excess of the costs of inspection to verify no work

having been done, based on $13 per inspector hour, the cost of

any engineering examination time previously devoted to

approval of plans based on $20 per hour, plus $19

administrative costs of "issuance and refund", shall be

refunded .............................................................. 19.00

done and when the District is satisfied that no more work will

be done under the permit, the fee in excess of the cost of any

engineering plans examination based on $20 per hour, cost of

inspections made, based on $13 per hour, plus $19

administrative costs of "issuance and refund", shall be

refunded .............................................................. 19.00

PENALTY

The penalty for a permit to abate notice of doing work without a permit shall be 50 percent of the fee.

WAIVER OF PERMIT FEES

No permit fee shall be charged when supported by evidence indicating that the applicant is under contract or subcontract to perform the following:

a
The Mayor of the District of Columbia shall amend from time to time the schedule of fees to be charged by the District of Columbia for the inspection of electrical equipment and for the issuance of permits to perform electrical services. The Mayor shall amend the schedule by rule to provide for fees in amounts as in his judgment will defray the approximate costs of performing inspections and issuing permits.
b
Until the schedule of fees is amended by the Mayor in accordance with subsection (a) of this section, the schedule of fees to be charged by the District of Columbia for the inspection of electrical equipment and for the issuance of permits to perform electrical services is as follows:
1
When no work has been done under authority of a permit,
2
When work authorized by permit has been only partially
3
Provided, that the request for refund shall be made within six months from the date of issuance and the permit and receipt are returned to the Permit Branch.
1
Work done exclusively for the District of Columbia.
2
Work done under contract for the District of Columbia.
3
Work done exclusively for agencies of the United States government.
