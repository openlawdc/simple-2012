The DC Code: § 31-3932.14 Standards and criteria applicable in a contested case brought by a third party and certain actions by the Commissioner.

Index → 31 Insurance and Securities. (Refs & Annos)
a
A contested case brought by a third party based on a decision of the Commissioner pursuant to this subchapter shall be governed by applicable civil law; provided, that, the aggrieved party shall:
1
Prove the appeal through clear and convincing evidence;
2
Demonstrate irreparable harm;
3
Not have another adequate remedy at law; and
4
Post a bond of sufficient surety to protect the interests of the holders of the SPFC securities and policyholders in an amount not less than 15% of the total amount of the securitized transaction.
b
If the Commissioner decides to reverse, amend, or modify a certificate of authority issued to an SPFC or the order issued in connection with them for a reason other than that specified in § 31-3931.15, the Commissioner shall meet the standards and criteria provided in subsection (a) of this section.
