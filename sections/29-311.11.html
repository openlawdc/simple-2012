The DC Code: § 29-311.11 Notice of intent to demand payment and consequences of voting or consenting.

Index → 29 Business Organizations. (Refs & Annos)
a
If a corporate action specified in § 29-311.02(a) is submitted to a vote at a shareholders' meeting, a shareholder that wishes to assert appraisal rights with respect to any class or series of shares shall:
1
Deliver to the corporation, before the vote is taken, written notice of the shareholder's intent to demand payment if the proposed action is effectuated; and
2
Not vote, or cause or permit to be voted, any shares of such class or series in favor of the proposed action.
b
If a corporate action specified in § 29-311.02(a) is to be approved by less than unanimous written consent, a shareholder that wishes to assert appraisal rights with respect to any class or series of shares shall not execute a consent in favor of the proposed action with respect to that class or series of shares.
c
A shareholder that fails to satisfy the requirements of subsection (a) or (b) of this section shall not be entitled to payment under this subchapter.
