The DC Code: § 22-3024 Privilege inapplicable for spouses or domestic partners.

Index → 22 Criminal Offenses and Penalties. (Refs & Annos)

Laws attaching a privilege against disclosure of communications between spouses or domestic partners are inapplicable in prosecutions under subchapter II of this chapter where the defendant is or was married to the victim, or is or was a domestic partner of the victim, or where the victim is a child.

Historical and Statutory

Prior Codifications 1981 Ed., § 22-4124. Effect of Amendments D.C. Law 16-306 rewrote the section, which had read as follows: "§ 22-3024. Spousal privilege inapplicable. "Laws attaching a privilege against disclosure of communications between a husband and wife are inapplicable in prosecutions under subchapter II of this chapter where the defendant is or was married to the victim or where the victim is a child."
