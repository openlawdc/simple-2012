The DC Code: § 46-219 No discrimination in employment for withholding.

Index → 46 Domestic Relations. (Refs & Annos)
a
No employer shall discharge, refuse to employ, take disciplinary action, or otherwise discriminate against any obligor for the reason that a party has subjected or attempted to subject unpaid earnings of the obligor to withholding or like proceedings for the purposes of paying support.
b
There shall be a rebuttable presumption that any employer who engages in conduct described in subsection (a) of this section, within 90 days from the date of receipt of a notice or order to withhold, is in violation of this subchapter and may be subject to the sanctions in subsection (c) of this section.
c
Any employer who engages in conduct described in subsection (a) of this section shall be subject to a civil penalty of up to $10,000.
d
Any civil penalty obtained under subsection (c) of this section shall be used to offset the obligor's duty of support.
