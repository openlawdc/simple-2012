The DC Code: § 7-626 Competency and intent of declarant.

Index → 7 Human Health Care and Safety. (Refs & Annos)
a
The desires of a qualified patient shall at all times supersede the effect of the declaration.
b
If the qualified patient is incompetent at the time of the decision to withhold or withdraw life-sustaining procedures, a declaration executed in accordance with § 7-622 is presumed to be valid. For the purpose of this subchapter, a physician or health facility may presume in the absence of actual notice to the contrary that an individual who executed a declaration was of sound mind when it was executed. The fact of an individual's having executed a declaration shall not be considered as an indication of a declarant's mental incompetency.
